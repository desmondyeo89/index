Nutrition
›
◆
Aim for a modest 300–400 kcal daily deficit. At 62.5 kg & 1.67 m, your TDEE (lightly active) is ~1,700 kcal — target ~1,300–1,400 kcal to lose 0.3–0.5 kg/week.
◆
Prioritise protein: 90–105 g/day (1.6 g/kg bodyweight) to protect muscle during your deficit. Focus on eggs, chicken, fish, Greek yoghurt, legumes.
◆
Fill half your plate with non-starchy vegetables at every meal — they're high volume, low calorie and keep you full.
◆
Minimise ultra-processed foods, sugary drinks and alcohol. These carry hidden calories and disrupt recovery.
◆
Don't skip meals to compensate for workouts — eat within 30–60 min after training to support recovery and prevent overeating later.
◆
Prep meals for 2–3 days ahead. Removing food decisions reduces impulsive choices.
💧
Hydration
›
◆
Drink 2.0–2.5 L of water daily at baseline. Add 500 ml for every 30 min of moderate exercise.
◆
Start each morning with 500 ml of water before coffee or food. This jump-starts metabolism and reduces false hunger.
◆
Drink 300–400 ml 30 minutes before runs to pre-hydrate without discomfort during the session.
◆
Urine colour is your best guide — aim for pale yellow. Dark yellow means you're under-hydrated.
◆
For runs over 45 min, consider electrolytes (sodium, potassium). Coconut water or a pinch of salt in water works well.
🌙
Sleep & Recovery
›
◆
Target 7.5–8.5 hours of sleep. Poor sleep elevates cortisol and ghrelin (hunger hormone) — making fat loss significantly harder.
◆
Stick to a consistent sleep/wake schedule — even on weekends. This regulates your circadian rhythm and improves sleep quality.
◆
Avoid screens 45 min before bed. Blue light delays melatonin release. Use Night Shift or blue-light glasses if needed.
◆
Keep your bedroom cool (18–20°C) and dark. These conditions promote deeper, more restorative sleep cycles.
◆
Rest days are not lazy days — muscles repair and grow during rest. Two rest days per week is built into your plan for this reason.
◆
If sore, try gentle walking or light stretching (active recovery) rather than full rest — it improves blood flow without stressing muscles.
⌚
Garmin Tips
›
◆
Use the Daily Suggested Workouts feature — Garmin calibrates intensity based on your training load and recovery status automatically.
◆
Track your Body Battery score each morning. If it's below 40, opt for a lighter workout or rest. Above 70 = green light to push.
◆
Enable Pace Alerts during runs to keep yourself in Zone 2 (easy conversational pace) — this is the optimal fat-burning zone for your current fitness level.
◆
Review your Stress Score daily. Elevated stress impacts recovery. On high-stress days, prioritise sleep over harder workouts.
◆
Set a step goal of 8,000–10,000 steps/day in Garmin Connect. Non-exercise movement (NEAT) contributes significantly to weekly calorie burn.
◆
Log your weight directly in Garmin Connect alongside this app for a full health picture and BMI tracking over time.
◆
Use Sleep Score data to identify patterns — late workouts, caffeine, and alcohol all reduce sleep quality as tracked by your Garmin's HRV data.
